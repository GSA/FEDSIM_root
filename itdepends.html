It Depends

July 17, 2018

Contracting Officers (COs) wield the pen that binds the Government. They take that responsibility very, very seriously, and they should. It is one of those jobs where ethical lapses can lead to jail time, and the only reward for doing a great job is to get more work assigned to you.

Have you ever met an 1102? I’m willing to bet that one of the first things out of their mouth after talking to them was a pause, and then: “It depends.”

Do you have kids? I have kids. How many of you have had one kid present the best version of the facts and conveniently leave out important context?

Contracting Officers (COs) wield the pen that binds the Government. They take that responsibility very, very seriously, and they should. It is one of those jobs where ethical lapses can lead to jail time, and the only reward for doing a great job is to get more work assigned to you.

Any good acquisition professional should answer with “it depends.” They should start with the underlying principle in the FAR Part 1:

...to deliver on a timely basis the best value product or service to the customer, while maintaining the public’s trust and fulfilling public policy objectives. Participants in the acquisition process should work together as a team and should be empowered to make decisions within their area of responsibility.

Who is on this “team?” There should be mix of financial, technical, and program folks. Most of these folks already have an idea of how they want to solve the problem, and let me tell you—it is urgent. They say, “We should just pick this one company and get started!” Meanwhile, the Contracting Officer says, “It depends.” The CO needs to gather all of the facts and consider a variety of factors before committing to a course of action:

  • How is the thing that we need sold? Is it commercial, or do we need to make it?
  • Is it something that a small business can provide?
  • Are there mandatory sources?
  • Did the technical people do a good job of describing their problem so that non-technical people understand it?
  • How busy am I right now?

Do you have kids? I have kids. How many of you have had one kid present the best version of the facts and conveniently leave out important context?

The best way for someone outside of the 1102 job series to be more successful in his or her job is to spend a few weeks in their shoes. Shadow or do a rotation in contracting—I guarantee that it will be far more fascinating that you think. You would be amazed at what the Government buys, how it buys, and how things work between companies. You will learn the all-important terms that we use and maybe, just maybe, get through your next contract with a little less pain.

Are you a Federal employee and want to try it out? Let us know of your interest and we’ll see if we can provide a detail.

Chris Hamm
Director, FEDSIM
